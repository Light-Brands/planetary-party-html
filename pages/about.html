About The Planetary Party

Meet the Weavers, Partners, and Visionaries

The Planetary Party is co-created by a diverse ecosystem of individuals, organizations, and communities committed to bioregional regeneration and collective celebration.

Core Stewardship Team

The Planetary Party is stewarded by a core team of organizers, weavers, and visionaries who hold the integrity of the ecosystem while supporting its distributed growth.

Photo of Syd
Harvey Griffith

Syd Harvey Griffith

Co-Founder & Executive Director

Syd Harvey Griffith is a community organizer and movement builder working at the intersection of grassroots action and regenerative finance. As Executive Director of Kinship Earth, Syd leads the organization's Bioregional Flow Funding efforts‚Äîshifting financial decision-making to grassroots leaders embedded in their communities.

This trust-based approach to giving provides flexible resources without grant applications or burdensome reporting requirements so that change-makers can focus on healing the places they call home.

Syd is also co-founder of nonprofit Permatours, a permaculture-action network that has supported 40+ land-based projects throughout the Northeast of Turtle Island through educational festivals and mutual aid‚Äîstrengthening local communities' capacity to provide affordable housing, healthy food, and infrastructure for events.

The Planetary Party is Syd's latest life purpose and passion project, where all of her worlds are converging. It's a federated ecosystem designing a community engagement process to weave regenerative networks and catalyze systemic transformation at a local level. The initiative's gatherings will serve as peak opportunities for communities to address pressing challenges, showcase local and global solutions, and unlock capital in service of people and planet.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a Planetary Party?

‚ñº

A Planetary Party is a bioregional gathering that combines festival, civic assembly, and action lab. It's where communities sense together, make decisions, celebrate through art and culture, and build regenerative infrastructure that endures.

How is this different from other festivals?

‚ñº

Unlike events that fade when tents come down, Planetary Parties leave behind positive-trace infrastructure‚Äîgardens, governance models, water systems, Flow Funds, and strengthened community capacity. Plus, they're part of a global learning network.

Can anyone host a Planetary Party?

‚ñº

Anyone can be inspired by the Planetary Party meme and host gatherings in that spirit. To host an official Party using the full Protocol and receiving Guild support, communities go through an onboarding process to ensure alignment and readiness.

What are Flow Funds?

‚ñº

Flow Funds are trust-based, community-governed pools of capital that move quickly to grassroots leaders and projects based on bioregional priorities. No grant applications or burdensome reporting‚Äîjust relational accountability and rapid deployment.

Do I need to be part of a bioregion to participate?

‚ñº

Everyone lives in a bioregion! You can participate by joining a Guild, volunteering at gatherings, amplifying the meme, or connecting us with your local community and networks.

How is the Planetary Party funded?

‚ñº

Through a combination of philanthropic partnerships, impact investments, bioregional Flow Fund allocations, event revenues, and individual donations. We're building regenerative economic models that support the work.

Is this a nonprofit?

‚ñº

The Planetary Party operates as a federated ecosystem. Core initiatives are fiscally sponsored through nonprofit partners like Kinship Earth, while the broader meme and framework are open-source and available to all.

How can I stay updated?

‚ñº

Join our newsletter, follow us on social media, and check this website for updates on gatherings, Guild opportunities, and bioregional activations.
