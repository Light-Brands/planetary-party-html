Pilot Bioregional Communities

Grounded in Place, Connected Globally

The Planetary Party Protocol is not a theory—it's a living practice finding its roots in bioregional communities around the world. These pilot bioregions are testing, refining, and demonstrating what regenerative transformation looks like.

The Planetary Party Protocol Finds Its Roots in Bioregional Communities

Each pilot bioregion is unique—shaped by its ecology, culture, and community. Yet all are united by a commitment to sense, decide, and act together in service to life.

What Makes a Pilot Bioregion?

Pilot bioregions are communities that:

  • Commit to implementing the full Planetary Party Protocol
  • Host a Planetary Party gathering (festival, assembly, action lab)
  • Develop a Bioregional Intelligence Dashboard
  • Activate a local Flow Fund
  • Build positive-trace infrastructure
  • Share learnings with the global network

Each activation feeds data, stories, and frameworks back into a shared knowledge commons, making replication easier.

Costa Rica: The Pura Vida Bioregion

About the Bioregion

Costa Rica is a global leader in ecological regeneration, with over 25% of its land protected and a commitment to carbon neutrality. Yet challenges remain: inequality, development pressures, and the need for economic models that truly serve life.

The Costa Rica Planetary Party brings together indigenous communities, permaculture projects, regenerative tourism initiatives, artists, and innovators to co-create a bioregional future.

Community Partners

▼
  • Indigenous councils and territories
  • Permaculture and regenerative agriculture networks
  • Eco-villages and intentional communities
  • Conservation organizations
  • Cultural and arts collectives
  • Youth and education initiatives

Focus Areas

▼
  • Watershed restoration and water sovereignty
  • Regenerative food systems and seed saving
  • Indigenous land rights and cultural preservation
  • Regenerative tourism models
  • Youth leadership and education
  • Bioregional governance frameworks

Southern California: The Bioregion of Transformation

About the Bioregion

Southern California is a landscape of contrasts—ecological abundance and scarcity, immense wealth and deep inequality, innovation and extraction. From the mountains to the sea, this bioregion is home to diverse ecosystems and communities working toward regeneration.

The Southern California Planetary Party weaves together indigenous nations, regenerative practitioners, social justice movements, artists, technologists, and visionaries committed to healing this place.

Community Partners

▼
  • Tongva and Chumash nations
  • Urban farming and food justice networks
  • Regenerative design and building collectives
  • Social and environmental justice organizations
  • Technology and innovation hubs
  • Arts and cultural movements

Focus Areas

▼
  • Water resilience and watershed restoration
  • Urban agriculture and food sovereignty
  • Indigenous land back and rematriation
  • Affordable housing and community land trusts
  • Renewable energy and climate adaptation
  • Healing justice and trauma integration

The Andes: Mountains of Wisdom

About the Bioregion

The Andes bioregion is home to some of the world's most ancient wisdom traditions and biodiverse ecosystems. Indigenous communities have stewarded these lands for millennia, maintaining practices that regenerate rather than extract.

An emerging Planetary Party activation is exploring how to honor and amplify these traditions while supporting communities facing pressures from development, climate change, and cultural erosion.

Potential Focus Areas

▼
  • Indigenous knowledge preservation and transmission
  • Sacred site protection and stewardship
  • Traditional agriculture and seed sovereignty
  • Watershed and glacier protection
  • Cultural tourism and economic sovereignty
  • Youth and elder knowledge exchange

Bring the Planetary Party to Your Bioregion

Is your community ready to host a Planetary Party? We're looking for bioregions with:

Prerequisites

  • Community readiness: Local leadership and broad participation
  • Bioregional focus: Clear ecological and cultural boundaries
  • Commitment to protocol: Willingness to implement all five phases
  • Alignment with values: Equity, ecology, sovereignty, joy
  • Capacity to convene: Ability to organize gatherings safely
  • Knowledge sharing: Commitment to documenting and sharing learnings

What We Provide

  • Planetary Party Protocol frameworks and training
  • Guild support and expertise
  • Dashboard development assistance
  • Flow Fund design and seed capital (for qualified bioregions)
  • Event production templates and best practices
  • Storytelling and media amplification
  • Connection to global network of partners

Application Process

  1. Submit initial expression of interest
  2. Participate in exploratory dialogue
  3. Community sensing and readiness assessment
  4. Co-create activation plan with local leadership
  5. Onboarding and training phase
  6. Begin Protocol implementation
